Quick-start guide
Aging Parent Admin Toolkit
Welcome to the Aging Parent Admin Toolkit. This guide walks you through how to set it up in about 2 hours.
Step 1 — Start with the emergency sheet (doc 1)
Fill in personal details and emergency contacts first. This is the most urgent document — needed in a crisis and takes 10 minutes to complete.
Print one copy and keep it visible at your parent's home (e.g., on the fridge). Share the digital version with all siblings.
Step 2 — Fill in the medical documents (docs 2–4)
Gather medication bottles and insurance cards. Fill in the medication log (doc 3) and provider directory (doc 4) from these sources.
Complete the medical history tracker (doc 2) with your parent's input — they'll know past diagnoses and surgeries best.
Bring the medication log to the next pharmacy visit to verify everything is current.
Step 3 — Tackle the legal & financial checklist (doc 5)
Work through this with your parent present. Focus first on whether a will and power of attorney exist.
If key documents are missing, prioritize contacting an elder law attorney. Many offer free 30-minute consultations.
Step 4 — Set up the coordination log (doc 7)
Share the sibling coordination log with everyone involved in care. Agree on a shared folder (Google Drive works well).
Use the appointment notes template (doc 6) before every doctor visit. Take it with you and write notes during the appointment.
How to store & share securely
Digital: Save to a shared Google Drive or Dropbox folder accessible to all family caregivers. Name the folder "Mom / Dad Care Documents."
Physical: Keep a printed binder at your parent's home with divider tabs for each section.
Security: Do not email documents containing SSNs or financial account numbers. Use password-protected Drive/Dropbox sharing instead.
Keeping it updated
Set a reminder every 3 months to review the medication log, provider directory, and emergency sheet. A 15-minute quarterly review keeps the toolkit accurate when it matters most.
